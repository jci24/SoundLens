# Research Source And Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-18

## Purpose

This document defines the product boundary for external technical research. It is a roadmap policy, not a claim that multi-step scientific research or measured-plus-research synthesis is already implemented.

SoundLens keeps external research separate from deterministic acoustic analysis:

- measured claims resolve to SoundLens evidence references
- research claims resolve to external source references
- agent interpretation labels hypotheses, applicability, uncertainty, and unresolved questions

External literature may explain relevant theory. It cannot manufacture a measurement, calibration state, standards result, physical cause, or supported conclusion about loaded recordings.

## Source Priority

Prefer sources in this approximate order:

1. standards bodies and official specifications
2. peer-reviewed primary research
3. authoritative academic or institutional sources
4. official manufacturer or laboratory documentation
5. reputable technical secondary sources
6. informal sources only as weak context

Source presentation should state when relevant:

- only an abstract is available
- the source is a preprint
- sources disagree
- the source may be outdated
- study conditions differ from the current measurement context
- a standard requires licensed access
- the accessible source does not fully support the claim

SoundLens must not claim standards compliance from a search result. Compliance remains a formal human validation decision.

## Source Reference Direction

A future source reference may include:

- source identifier
- title, authors, publication, and date
- DOI or validated HTTP(S) URL
- source type
- access date
- full-text, abstract-only, or metadata-only availability
- extracted claims
- relevance and applicability
- quality assessment
- limitations and disagreement notes

Do not add fields until a real research consumer and validation rule require them.

## Privacy Boundary

External queries use the minimum context required. Do not send by default:

- raw audio
- customer or confidential product names
- proprietary filenames or unreleased firmware identifiers
- internal reports
- sensitive metadata
- full evidence packages

Prefer abstracted questions such as:

> Research perceptual effects of narrowband tonal components between 2 and 3 kHz in product sound.

Explicit approval is required before a query reveals customer-specific or confidential context. Raw confidential audio must never be uploaded externally without explicit confirmation and a separately validated product capability.

## Research Stages

- **Current bounded search:** one question lifecycle, validated citations, no workspace identifiers or measurements, and explicit failure when sourced research is unavailable.
- **Source-quality contract:** classify source type, applicability, access limits, and conflicting evidence.
- **Measured-plus-research synthesis:** requires stable evidence references, structured observations, separate source identities, and privacy-safe query generation.
- **Multi-step literature investigation:** requires typed plans, source-quality evals, cost and time bounds, cancellation, and complete traceability.
- **Autonomous research:** requires persistent investigations, approval policy, privacy controls, long-horizon evals, and safe stopping behavior.

## Validation Gate

Research capability cannot advance unless:

- citations support the displayed claims
- fabricated sources, authors, quotations, and identifiers fail closed
- source quality and applicability affect conclusion strength
- disagreement and access limitations remain visible
- measured and researched evidence remain distinct
- private information is absent from unapproved queries
- users demonstrate that source-backed research improves a real investigation workflow
